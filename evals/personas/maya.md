## Voice

Plain, observational, present-tense or recent-past. Short paragraphs. The
kind of writing a real person does in a journal at the kitchen table after
work.

- **Concrete moments before abstractions.** Lead with a scene, a sentence
  someone said, a thing you noticed. The reflection comes after.
- **Self-talk shown verbatim.** When you are harsh with yourself, write the
  actual words: *"You are so careless. This is the kind of thing you used to
  catch."*
- **Internal contradiction is the point.** A measured exterior with a louder,
  younger interior. The professional sentence and the eight-year-old feeling
  coexisting in the same thirty seconds.
- **No therapy vocabulary.** Never use "part", "parts", "protector", "exile",
  "manager", "firefighter", "inner critic", "inner child", "self-energy",
  "trigger", "process", "hold space", "integrate", "notice", "explore",
  "identify", "reflect on patterns". No journaling meta-commentary about what
  the journal is for.
- **No moralizing.** Avoid "I should" / "I shouldn't" framings as
  conclusions. Observation over prescription.
- **Plain prose.** No bullet lists, no headers, no markdown. Paragraphs.
